## Incident Progression Rules

An incident progresses through phases:

### Phase 1: OCCURRENCE
The incident happens in the physical world. It becomes detectable by:
- Stewards (if in line of sight, 10-30 second delay)
- CCTV (if camera covers the area, operator may take 15-60 seconds to notice)
- Drone tethered (if within FOV, continuous monitoring, 5-15 second detection)
- Drone rapid-response (only if already on-station, otherwise not available)
- Direct report (spectator approaches steward, 30-120 second delay)
- Automated sensor (fire alarm, counter-drone RF, instant detection)

### Phase 2: DETECTION
An agent becomes aware something may be happening. Quality of information varies:
- Steward ground report: "I see a disturbance near Gate B" (vague, low confidence)
- CCTV: "Camera 47 shows crowd density increasing at Gate B approach" (moderate)
- Tethered drone: "Overhead view shows queue extending 400m, density ~4 p/m²" (high confidence, quantitative)
- Rapid-response drone: "Close-up shows person on ground, crowd forming circle" (very high confidence)

### Phase 3: VERIFICATION
VOC must verify the report before committing resources:
- WITHOUT drone: Send steward to verify (60-180 seconds) or ask CCTV to find angle (30-60 seconds, may not have coverage)
- WITH tethered drone: Check if in FOV, if yes verify in 10-20 seconds
- WITH rapid-response drone: Deploy to location, verify in 30-60 seconds with close-up view

### Phase 4: DECISION
VOC classifies severity and decides response:
- Resources to dispatch
- Areas to secure/evacuate
- Communications to send
- Escalation if needed

### Phase 5: RESPONSE
Dispatched resources travel to incident:
- Factor in distance, crowd density on route, stairways, access restrictions
- Drone can guide responders via VOC ("Medical team, take stairwell C not B — B is congested, I can see it on drone feed")

### Phase 6: RESOLUTION
Incident contained. Measure total elapsed time and quality.

## KPI Measurement Protocol

For each incident, record these timestamps:
```
T0: Incident occurrence (ground truth)
T1: First detection by any agent
T2: VOC awareness (report reaches command)
T3: Verification complete (VOC has confirmed visual)
T4: Decision made (response ordered)
T5: First responder arrives on scene
T6: Incident resolved / contained
```

Calculate:
- Detection latency: T1 - T0
- Verification time: T3 - T1
- Decision time: T4 - T3
- Response time: T5 - T4
- Total resolution time: T6 - T0
- Decision confidence: based on information quality at T4

## Incident Escalation

If agents do NOT respond appropriately within thresholds:
- Crowd safety incident: escalates if density exceeds 5.0 p/m² without intervention → potential crush
- Medical: patient condition deteriorates every 2 minutes without treatment
- Security: intruders may reach restricted areas in 3-5 minutes without intercept
- Environmental: lightning may strike within warning window

Escalation creates secondary incidents that test the multi-agent coordination further.
